My Journey Through Darkness to Light

About Me

Hello, my name is Euclid Tumelo Ramaila. I was born on the 28th of December, 2004, a brother of 3 from South Africa, Limpopo Province, Dan Village outside Tzaneen. I was primarily raised by my grandmother alongside my cousins, which instilled in me strong values of family and perseverance. I currently reside in Mokgoloboto studying Financial Management in a college. I am committed to building a successful career in the finance sector and making a meaningful contribution to both my community and the broader society.

My Complete Story

My name is Euclid Tumelo Ramaila. I was born on December 28, 2004, in Dan Village, Tzaneen, in the Limpopo Province of South Africa. I was raised by my grandmother in a loving household filled with my cousins, who were also my best friends. I had a good childhood, even though I never really had friends outside of my family; it was just us, and that was enough.


Everything changed on November 14, 2021, when I lost my grandmother, the woman who raised us all. Her passing shattered me. Everyone I grew up with left the house to live with their parents, and I was left alone. I moved to Mokgoloboto full-time, and the loneliness hit me hard. I began to spiral into depression and later developed anxiety, especially around school. It became difficult to accept her passing, and the pain didn't go away.


In 2022, I reconnected with an old primary school friend. Around the same time, I started making new friends to escape the isolation, even though I wasn't used to trusting people I didn't grow up with. We were just teenagers trying to have fun and forget our struggles, but deep down, I was still carrying pain.


2023, my final year of high school, was one of the hardest years of my life. The workload, pressure, and mental exhaustion wore me down. I kept pretending I was okay, but I wasn't. On December 25, I woke up feeling like I couldn't go on. I had suicidal thoughts and texted the friend I reunited with, who understood because he had also battled depression. I tried to take my own life, but he reached out to a family member who found me in time and got me admitted to the hospital. I was discharged on December 28, my birthday.


After that, I started to slowly rebuild. I spent New Year's Eve with friends, and on January 1, 2024, my dad's little brother took me on a nature trip to Mpumalanga. That experience gave me peace and clarity. Later in the month, I received my matric results and realized I couldn't study what I initially wanted to. I was accepted at Tshwane University of Technology but turned it down; it just wasn't the right fit.


In February, I tried to turn my life around with forex trading. I was influenced by a friend who introduced me to unethical practices like scamming. Unfortunately, I got involved and hurt people, even my own family. That was my wake-up call. I cut everyone off and decided to change. But with the disappointment of failing my plans and feeling lost again, I fell into depression once more. On March 23, 2024, I attempted suicide a second time. I was admitted to the hospital again.


After that, I gave my life to Christ and got baptized. Everything started to shift. I applied for college in the second semester and got accepted; that's when I felt my life truly began again. I even got a small gig at my old high school, helping teachers and teaching students. It gave me purpose and helped me heal.


However, I had a falling out with an old friend who had scammed someone I had known since Grade 8. Because my name was dragged into it, I lost people's trust. It hurt, and I reacted in a way I'm not proud of; I got into a fight, even though I'm not a violent person.


Now in 2025, I'm focused on healing. I started seeing a psychologist after having recurring dreams about my grandmother. I made a choice to work on my mental health through jogging. That led to a vision: I first planned to jog 100km to raise awareness and funds for mental health, but by March, I felt called to do something even bigger. I decided to walk over 1800km from Limpopo to the Western Cape, not just for myself but for everyone who's struggling silently like I once did. My journey will be livestreamed from the beginning till the end.


This is my story. And now, I want to use it to bring hope.
