Who I Am

About Me

Hello, my name is Euclid Tumelo Ramaila. I was born on the 28th of December, 2004 — a brother of three from South Africa, Limpopo Province, Dan Village outside Tzaneen. I was primarily raised by my grandmother alongside my cousins, which instilled in me strong values of family and perseverance.

I currently reside in Mokgoloboto, studying Financial Management at college. I am committed to building a successful career in the finance sector while making a meaningful contribution to my community and society through mental health advocacy.

This website — Journey4Change — was built with my own hands to break the stigma around mental health in South Africa. What started as a plan to jog 100 km became a calling to walk 3 500 km across all 9 provinces. This is my story, and I'm sharing it so you know you are not alone.

The Journey

Five Chapters That Changed Everything

01
2021

Loss

Everything shifted on November 14, 2021 — the day I lost my grandmother, the woman who had been the heart and anchor of our family. Her passing didn't just break me; it fractured the very foundation of our lives. Without her, we scattered in different directions, each of us forced to leave the only home we had ever known. In the silence that followed, I found myself standing alone, surrounded by memories that no longer had a place to belong.

02
2022

Depression

I began to spiral into depression and later developed anxiety, especially around school. I started hanging out with a group of friends thinking the loneliness would go away — but the company made things worse. I reconnected with an old primary school friend and tried to rebuild. We were just teenagers trying to have fun and forget our struggles, but deep down I was still carrying enormous pain I couldn't name.

03
2023 — Crisis

The Edge

My final year of high school was one of the hardest of my life. The workload, the pressure, the mental exhaustion wore me down completely. I kept pretending I was okay, but I wasn't. On December 25, I woke up feeling like I couldn't go on. I had suicidal thoughts and texted my friend, who understood — he had battled depression too. I tried to take my own life, but he reached out to a family member who found me in time. I was admitted to hospital and discharged on December 28.

04
2024

Transformation

After a second attempt in March — and another hospital stay — I gave my life to Christ and got baptized. Everything started to shift. I applied for college in the second semester and got accepted. I got a small gig at my old high school, helping teachers and teaching students. It gave me purpose and helped me heal. For the first time in years, I felt like my life had truly begun again.

05
2025

Purpose

Now focused on healing, I started seeing a psychologist and took up jogging. That led to a vision — first 100 km, then something bigger. By March, I felt called to walk 3 500 km across all 9 provinces of South Africa, not just for myself but for everyone who is struggling silently like I once was. Today, Journey4Change is born — a platform to help people open up, get professional help, and know they are not alone.

In His Own Words

My Complete Story

My name is Euclid Tumelo Ramaila. I was born on December 28, 2004, in Dan Village, Tzaneen, in the Limpopo Province of South Africa. I was raised by my grandmother in a loving household filled with my cousins, who were also my best friends. I had a good childhood, even though I never really had friends outside of my family — it was just us, and that was enough.

Everything changed on November 14, 2021, when I lost my grandmother, the woman who raised us all. Her passing shattered me. Everyone I grew up with left the house to live with their parents, and I was left alone. I moved to Mokgoloboto full-time, and the loneliness hit me hard. I began to spiral into depression and later developed anxiety, especially around school. It became difficult to accept her passing, and the pain didn't go away.

The beginning of 2022 I started hanging out with a group of friends, thinking the loneliness would go away — but the friendship I had with those people made things worse for the years I was associated with them. When schools opened, I reconnected with an old primary school friend. We were just teenagers trying to have fun and forget our struggles, but deep down I was still carrying pain.

2023, my final year of high school, was one of the hardest years of my life. The workload, pressure, and mental exhaustion wore me down. I kept pretending I was okay, but I wasn't. On December 25, I woke up feeling like I couldn't go on. I had suicidal thoughts and texted the friend I had reconnected with. I tried to take my own life, but he reached out to a family member who found me in time and got me admitted to hospital. I was discharged on December 28.

After that, I started to slowly rebuild. I spent New Year's Eve with friends back home, and on January 1, 2024, my dad's little brother took me on a nature trip to Mpumalanga. That experience gave me peace and clarity. In February, I got involved in forex trading through a friend who introduced me to unethical practices. I hurt people — even my own family. That was my wake-up call. I cut everyone off and decided to change. On March 23, 2024, I attempted suicide a second time. I was admitted to hospital again.

After that, I gave my life to Christ and got baptized. Everything started to shift. I applied for college in the second semester and got accepted. I even got a small gig at my old high school, helping teachers and teaching students. It gave me purpose and helped me heal.

Now in 2025, I'm focused on healing. I started seeing a psychologist after having recurring dreams about my grandmother. I made a choice to work on my mental health through jogging — and that led to a vision. First I planned to jog 100 km to raise awareness and funds for mental health, but by March I felt called to do something even bigger: a 3 500 km journey across all 9 provinces — not just for myself, but for everyone who is struggling silently like I once was.

Today Journey4Change is born — a platform to help people open up and get professional help. With this website I built, I want to break the stigma. This is my story. And now, I want to use it to bring hope.

"Suicide is a permanent solution to a temporary problem. You are not alone."

If my story resonated with you, join the movement — your voice matters.
