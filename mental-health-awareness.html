The Facts

Understanding Mental Health & Suicide

Depression and anxiety are closely linked to an increased risk of suicide. While not everyone with these conditions will experience suicidal thoughts, they are significant risk factors. Studies estimate that about 60% of people who die by suicide have had a mood disorder such as depression or bipolar disorder.

Anxiety disorders can also contribute to suicidal ideation, especially when combined with other stressors. Recognising the signs early and seeking help is the most powerful step anyone can take.

Breaking the Silence

The Importance of Open Conversations

Stigma often prevents individuals from seeking help for mental health issues. Initiatives like "No Shame Day" encourage people to share their mental health experiences openly, aiming to reduce stigma and promote healing.

Public figures and ordinary people alike are stepping forward to share their stories — reminding us all that mental health struggles do not discriminate, and that asking for help is an act of strength, not weakness.

Tools for Healing

Coping Strategies That Work

Psychological Strategies

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) Helps you recognise and reframe negative thought patterns.
  • Mindfulness & Meditation Practising presence reduces anxiety and brings emotional balance.
  • Journaling Writing feelings helps process emotions and identify patterns.
  • Breathing Exercises Deep, intentional breathing calms the nervous system immediately.

Physical & Lifestyle

  • Exercise Regular activity releases endorphins and reduces stress hormones.
  • Sleep Hygiene A consistent sleep routine improves mood and brain function.
  • Nutrition Balanced meals support brain health — omega-3s, B vitamins, magnesium.
  • Limit Substance Use Alcohol and drugs worsen symptoms of depression and anxiety.

Social Support & Connection

  • Support Groups Talking with others who understand brings incredible relief.
  • Trusted Friends or Mentors Reaching out reduces isolation — you don't have to carry this alone.
  • Volunteering Helping others builds purpose and shifts focus away from pain.
  • Online Communities Peer support forums offer anonymous, understanding spaces.

Spiritual & Emotional

  • Prayer or Spiritual Reflection Connecting to a higher power brings comfort and hope for many.
  • Gratitude Practice Daily gratitude journaling slowly shifts your perspective over time.
  • Affirmations Repeating positive phrases can rewire negative thought cycles.
  • Professional Help Therapists and counsellors are trained to walk this journey with you.
