Mental Health Awareness

Understanding the Link Between Mental Health and Suicide

Depression and anxiety are closely linked to an increased risk of suicide. While not everyone with these conditions will experience suicidal thoughts, they are significant risk factors. Studies estimate that about 60% of people who die by suicide have had a mood disorder such as depression or bipolar disorder. Anxiety disorders can also contribute to suicidal ideation, especially when combined with other stressors.

Breaking the Silence: The Importance of Open Conversations

Stigma often prevents individuals from seeking help for mental health issues. Initiatives like "No Shame Day," launched by The Siwe Project, encourage people to share their mental health experiences openly, aiming to reduce stigma and promote healing. Similarly, public figures like Colton Underwood have shared their personal struggles with depression and anxiety to highlight the importance of seeking help and eliminating shame.

Strategies for Coping and Seeking Support

  • Talk About It: Sharing your feelings with trusted friends, family, or mental health professionals can provide relief and perspective.
  • Professional Help: Therapists and counsellors are trained to help you navigate mental health challenges. Finding someone you trust is crucial for effective therapy.
  • Community and Routine: Engaging in community activities and establishing daily routines can provide structure and support.
Healthline Mental Health Tips

Psychological Strategies

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT): Helps you recognize and reframe negative thought patterns.
  • Mindfulness & Meditation: Practicing being present can reduce anxiety and bring emotional balance.
  • Journaling: Writing your thoughts and feelings can help process emotions and identify patterns.
  • Breathing Exercises: Deep, intentional breathing can calm your nervous system.

Physical & Lifestyle Strategies

  • Exercise: Regular physical activity releases endorphins and reduces stress hormones.
  • Sleep Hygiene: Creating a sleep routine improves mood and brain function.
  • Nutrition: Balanced meals support brain health â€” omega-3s, B vitamins, and magnesium are especially helpful.
  • Limiting Substance Use: Alcohol and drugs can worsen symptoms of depression and anxiety.

Social Support & Connection

  • Support Groups: Talking to others who understand what you're going through can be incredibly healing.
  • Trusted Friends or Mentors: Reaching out to someone you trust can reduce isolation.
  • Online Communities: Forums or social media groups focused on mental health offer anonymous peer support.
  • Volunteering: Helping others can build purpose and shift focus away from your own pain.

Spiritual & Emotional Practices

  • Prayer or Spiritual Reflection: For many, connecting to a higher power brings comfort and hope.
  • Gratitude Practice: Listing things you're grateful for each day can slowly shift your perspective.
  • Affirmations: Repeating positive, self-loving phrases can rewire negative thought cycles.
